The textbook definition of 3D rendering refers to taking a 3 dimensional wireframe

model and generating a 2 dimensional photographic image. Typically this is done

with artistic lighting, surface representations, and shadows that enhance a simulated

solid object. This technology has evolved in the past 10 years. It is being used on

scales we can’t even imagine in cinematography and animated movies. This artistic

rendering skill has a place in the automotive and consumer product industry. Below

are a few images of current and upcoming projects that make use of 3D rendering

to aid in selling a product design and technical molding concept to our customers.
